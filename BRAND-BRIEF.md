# VANTIS MOTORS — BRAND BRIEF

---

## BRAND IDENTITY

### Company Overview
- **Company Name:** Vantis Motors
- **Tagline:** "Beyond Elevation"
- **Founded:** Denver, Colorado
- **Brand Essence:** Born from the belief that the mountains aren't a place to escape from — they're a place to become who you are.
- **Core Promise:** Electric vehicles engineered for people who need their vehicle to keep up with their ambition. Rugged enough for a winter pass. Refined enough for a boardroom. Zero compromises. Zero emissions.

### Mission Statement
To build the vehicles that ambitious people deserve — machines that refuse to choose between performance and responsibility, between wilderness and refinement, between the earth and the future.

### Brand Personality
- **Confident** — Commands respect without asking for it
- **Purposeful** — Every detail earns its place
- **Bold** — Stands apart from the electric mainstream
- **Grounded** — Rooted in real mountain culture, not marketing copy
- **Premium** — Not flashy. Genuinely exceptional.

### Brand Voice
- Direct and evocative. No corporate speak.
- Confident declarations, not hedged claims.
- Appeals to ambition, capability, and self-determination.
- Poetic when describing the experience, precise when describing the engineering.

### Typography

**Primary Heading Font:** Inter (Google Fonts)
- **Usage:** All headings, navigation, CTAs
- **Weights:** 700 (Bold), 600 (SemiBold), 400 (Regular)
- **Character:** Clean, authoritative, modern without being sterile

**Secondary / Body Font:** Inter
- **Usage:** Body copy, feature descriptions, specs
- **Weight:** 400 (Regular), 300 (Light for long-form)
- **Line Height:** 1.6 for body, 1.2 for headings

**Display / Hero Font:** Inter
- **Usage:** Hero headlines, model names
- **Treatment:** Large scale, tight letter-spacing (-0.02em to -0.04em), uppercase for model designations

**Monospace (specs/data):** JetBrains Mono or SF Mono
- **Usage:** Numeric specs, technical data, charging stats

### Typography Scale
- Hero H1: 72px / 600 weight / -0.03em tracking
- Page H1: 56px / 600 weight / -0.02em tracking
- Page H2: 40px / 600 weight / -0.02em tracking
- Section H3: 28px / 600 weight / -0.01em tracking
- Body Large: 20px / 400 weight / 1.6 line-height
- Body: 16px / 400 weight / 1.6 line-height
- Caption / Label: 13px / 500 weight / 0.05em tracking / uppercase

### Design Principles
- **Dark-first aesthetic** — Dark backgrounds make the amber accent feel earned, not decorative
- **Editorial minimalism** — Generous whitespace, nothing fights for attention
- **Full-bleed imagery** — Hero sections and model reveals use edge-to-edge photography
- **Asymmetric layouts** — Hero text offset left or right, not centered and predictable
- **Motion restraint** — Subtle fade-ins and scroll reveals. No spinning, bouncing, or distraction
- **Gold as punctuation** — Not a fill color. Used for lines, icons, hover states, key data points

Vantis was born on a ski lift.

## DESIGN NOTES

### Layout Principles
- **12-column grid** with generous gutters (24px–32px)
- **Full-bleed hero sections** — 100vw, dark background, amber headline accent
- **Asymmetric hero text** — headline offset left or right, not centered
- **Sticky navigation** — transparent over hero, solid dark on scroll
- **Section padding** — 120px top/bottom on desktop, 80px on mobile
- **Specs tables** — full-width, alternating row backgrounds (#1a1a1a / #242424)

### Imagery Direction
- **Dark, dramatic lighting** — golden hour on mountain passes, deep shadows, cinematic
- **Vehicles in environment** — not on white backgrounds or in studios. In the mountains. On the road. In context.
- **No lifestyle stock photos** — if used, must be authentic and editorial, not aspirational-advertising
- **Vehicle on dark backgrounds** — #0a0a0a or #1a1a1a for studio shots
- **Accent color in images** — amber highlights in lighting, brake calipers, interior trim stitching

### Motion
- **Scroll-triggered fade-ins** — opacity 0→1, translateY 20px→0, 600ms ease-out
- **Navigation transitions** — 200ms ease for color/background changes
- **CTA hover** — amber background shift (#c9a84c → #d4b860), subtle scale(1.02), 150ms
- **Hero text** — staggered reveal: headline first, then body, then CTAs (100ms delay between)
- **No parallax** — feels dated; instead use subtle scale on scroll (1.0 → 0.98 as user scrolls)
- **Image load** — blur-up placeholder → full image (low-res base64 → high-res)

### Responsive Strategy
- **Breakpoints:** 1280px (desktop), 1024px (tablet), 768px (large mobile), 375px (small mobile)
- **Hero text** — scales from 72px → 48px → 36px → 28px across breakpoints
- **Specs tables** — horizontal scroll on mobile, sticky first column
- **Navigation** — full desktop nav → hamburger overlay on tablet/mobile
- **Images** — srcset responsive images, art-directed crops for mobile vs desktop

### Accessibility
- **Color contrast** — All text meets WCAG AA on dark backgrounds
- **Focus states** — amber outline on all interactive elements
- **Alt text** — descriptive alt text on all images, "Image: [vehicle name] on mountain road"
- **Skip nav** — "Skip to main content" link on page load
- **Reduced motion** — respects `prefers-reduced-motion` — disables scroll animations

## CONTENT PRINCIPLES

1. **No "we're excited" language** — No "We're thrilled to introduce..." No "We're proud to announce..." Write like the vehicle speaks for itself.
2. **Specific over general** — "420 miles" beats "incredible range." "4.1 seconds" beats "fast."
3. **Active voice always** — "The APEX delivers" not "The APEX was designed to deliver."
4. **Feature → Benefit** — Don't just describe what's there. Describe what it means for the person driving.
5. **No asterisks** — Range figures are EPA estimates. Performance numbers are verified. No fine print hiding the truth.
6. **Write for skimmers** — Bold the key fact. Use short paragraphs. Let the design carry the weight, not the prose.
